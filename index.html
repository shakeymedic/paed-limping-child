pGALS Screening (Paediatric Gait, Arms, Legs, Spine)

Gait

Antalgic / Trendelenburg? Tip-toe walking, then heel walking.

Arms

Restricted range of movement, stiffness, swelling, erythema.

Legs

Bruising, deformity, erythema, pelvis level, leg lengths equal? Feel for knee effusion/warmth; passive/active knee flexion with IR/ER of hip — compare IR of both hips (restricted IR is a sensitive sign of hip pathology).

Spine

Observe from side and behind; ask child to touch toes.

⚠️ >38.5°C — fever flag

Reduced internal rotation suggests intra-articular hip pathology (septic arthritis, transient synovitis, SUFE, Perthes).

4. Kocher Criteria — Septic Arthritis of the Hip

Four original Kocher (1999) criteria plus the Caird (2006) modification (CRP). Live probability calculated as each criterion is answered.

1. Fever >38.5°C (history or measured)
2. Non-weight bearing
3. ESR ≥40 mm/hr (if available)
4. WBC >12,000/mm³ (if available)
5. CRP >20 mg/L Caird Modification

Kocher Score

0 / 5

16.9% — Unlikely septic arthritis

Continue standard assessment; consider transient synovitis

⚠️ Septic arthritis can still be present in the absence of these criteria.

Management Trigger (Local Guideline)

If features consistent with septic arthritis (severe pain OR range of movement <75% of normal) → urgent orthopaedics referral.

5. Investigations

Joint Aspiration Note (Local Guideline)

If suspicion of transient synovitis or septic arthritis, perform joint aspiration with microscopy and culture — these CANNOT usually be differentiated by ultrasound and require laboratory and clinical correlation.

Investigations per Local Guideline

FBC + blood film, ESR, CRP, blood cultures if febrile. X-ray 2 views (site of pain + pelvis). If SUFE suspected: AP + frog lateral. If osteomyelitis / persisting symptoms: MRI pelvis ± contrast with paediatric radiologist, bone scan, CT (as addition to MRI). CK, sickle cell screen.

⚠️ >12,000 — Kocher criterion met

⚠️ >20 — Caird criterion met

⚠️ ≥40 — Kocher criterion met

6. Differential Diagnosis Checklist

0–3yr

  • Septic arthritis / osteomyelitis
  • Developmental hip dysplasia
  • Fracture / soft tissue (toddler's fractures / NAI)

3–10yr

  • Transient synovitis / irritable hip
  • Septic arthritis / osteomyelitis
  • Perthes disease
  • Fracture / soft tissue (stress fracture)

10–15yr

  • SUFE
  • Septic arthritis / osteomyelitis
  • Perthes disease
  • Fracture / soft tissue (stress fracture)

Other (All Ages)

  • Non-accidental injury (NAI) — all ages
  • Neoplastic (e.g. ALL)
  • Haematological (e.g. sickle cell)
  • Pyomyositis / discitis
  • Rickets
  • Neuromuscular (CP / muscular dystrophy)
  • Limb length inequality
  • JIA

Clinical Clues

Transient synovitis: Kocher 0-1, no fever, recent URTI, self-limiting

Septic arthritis: Systemically unwell, Kocher ≥3, urgent joint aspiration

Perthes: Insidious onset 4-8yr, decreased IR, avascular necrosis

SUFE: Obese adolescent, hip/knee pain, Trethowan sign on X-ray

Osteomyelitis: Fever, point tenderness over bone, elevated inflammatory markers

Leukaemia/malignancy: Night pain, weight loss, anaemia, lymphadenopathy — urgent haematology

Transient Synovitis — Key Points (Local Guideline)

  • Commonest atraumatic cause of limp; typically age 3–8yr; male predominance
  • Caution in <3yr — increased risk of NAI / septic arthritis; transient synovitis is rare — investigate ALL <3yr
  • Diagnosis of exclusion — always consider septic arthritis
  • Symptoms <48hr + systemically well + afebrile + able to weight bear → no further investigations necessary
  • Follow up at 48hr; investigate if symptoms persist
  • Age >8yr with risk factors for SUFE: further investigations including AP and frog lateral X-rays
